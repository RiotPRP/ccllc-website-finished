The Regulatory Inflection Point

The 2026–2027 VOC Mandate
Is Not Optional.

The EPA and OSHA are enforcing sweeping Volatile Organic Compound (VOC) emission restrictions across all industrial finishing operations. Beginning in 2026, facilities still operating liquid coating lines will face mandatory compliance audits, escalating fines, and — for repeat violations — operational shutdowns.

This is not a distant threat. It is a present operational emergency. The conversion window from liquid-to-powder finishing is measured in months, not years. Facilities that delay this transition will face regulatory penalties while their competitors — those who pivoted early with properly designed systems — operate leaner, cleaner, and with dramatically lower material costs.

The liquid-to-powder pivot requires more than purchasing a new oven. It demands a complete re-engineering of your process architecture: pre-treatment chemistry, substrate analysis, throughput mapping, cure verification, and compliance documentation. This is precisely what we do.
